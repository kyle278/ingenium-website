## Design Context

### Users
Ingenium's public site is aimed at startup founders, SME operators, and marketing teams who are trying to turn a fragmented website, CRM, automation, and AI stack into one connected growth system. They are usually evaluating vendors while dealing with lead leakage, inconsistent reporting, and operational drag, so the interface should feel credible quickly and explain capability without forcing them through jargon.

### Brand Personality
Premium, helpful, and knowledgeable. The tone should communicate calm competence and product maturity without becoming cold, overly technical, bubbly, or overly friendly. Buyers should feel that Ingenium understands operational detail and can guide them through it clearly.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light, premium, technical, and product-first. Use a warm mineral-white canvas, deep graphite text, and restrained blue/teal accents. Typography should feel deliberate, high-contrast, and quietly confident: refined enough for founder-level buying decisions, practical enough for marketing and operations teams, and never cyber-SaaS or playful startup-cute.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with clarity before flourish: hierarchy must make key claims, proof, and next actions legible at a glance.
2. Keep the tone precise and reassuring: premium typography should support trust, not showmanship.
3. Use contrast as structure: strong text contrast, clear weight shifts, and disciplined spacing should carry the interface.
4. Favor product confidence over agency theater: the site should read like an operational system with a polished front end, not like a consultancy mood board.
